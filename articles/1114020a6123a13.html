Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

After a strong start to the League One season at Burton Albion, striker Victor Adeboyejo could be on Hull City's radar in the January transfer window.

The 24-year-old has long been on City's radar, having previously been tracked at the MKM Stadium under Grant McCann's stewardship. Although no official bid was made during his time at Barnsley, the parties did make contact about a potential deal.

City are considering a move for a forward when the transfer window opens on January 1, with manager Liam Rosenior eager to add to his forward ranks and provide competition for Oscar Estupinan, Tyler Smith, and Benjamin Tetteh upon his return from injury, though he is aware that he must work smartly within financial fair play constraints.

Adeboyejo's goals for the Brewers this season have alerted a slew of potential suitors to his availability in January, having netted nine times in a struggling Albion side, plus a couple more in the EFL Trophy, while also assisting twice.




His five goals in seven games in October helped the Staffordshire-based side pick up eight points, including a hat-trick in the 3-2 win over Forest Green, in which he netted a header and a 20-yard curler.

His goals accounted for seven of Burton's eight points in the month, earning him a nomination for Player of the Month, which he narrowly lost to Bristol Rovers' Aaron Collins.

Since leaving Arsenal as a youngster, the frontman has had a nomadic existence, representing a variety of clubs and even having a trial with Chelsea in 2017 before spending five years at Barnsley.
