Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

Because of the colder weather, heating bills tend to rise in the winter, which can be difficult for the poor, especially given this year's energy crisis.

In an attempt to address this, the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) offers the cold weather payment, which provides people with extra money when temperatures fall below zero.

This payment predated the 650 cost-of-living payment, a staggered 400 energy bill rebate, and a boosted winter fuel payment for pensioners, all of which were implemented in response to cost-of-living difficulties.

With temperatures now below zero in much of the country, the cold weather payment may be put to use in the coming days.

The Met Office and the UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) have issued a severe cold weather alert for England, one level below a national emergency, warning that dangerously low temperatures could endanger vulnerable people's health and disrupt deliveries.

The cold weather payment is worth how much, when it is paid, and who is eligible to receive it.

The cold weather payment is a DWP program that runs from November 1 to March 31 each year.

Eligible people receive a 25 payment for every seven-day period when the temperature falls below zero, which is intended to be used for heating bills and other necessities.

You may be eligible for cold weather payments if you currently receive any of the following:

People receiving income support or income-based jobseeker's allowance may be eligible for cold weather payments if they have a disability or pensioner premium, a disabled child, a child tax credit with a disability component, or a child under the age of five living with them.




People who are eligible for ESA will also be eligible if they belong to a work-related activity or support group, receive a severe or enhanced disability premium, a pensioner premium, a child tax credit with a disability component, or have a child under the age of five living with them.

Universal credit claimants are also eligible if they are not employed or self-employed, have a health condition or disability that limits their ability to work, and/or have a child under the age of five living with them.

They will also be eligible if they have a disabled child, regardless of whether they are employed.

If you are eligible, enter your postcode into the DWP's website here to see if your area is cold enough for a payment.

If you are eligible for a payment, you should automatically receive it.

However, if your circumstances have changed and you believe you should now be eligible, for example, if you have a baby or a child under the age of five moves in with you, you should contact Jobcentre Plus.

If you believe you should have received a cold weather payment but did not, contact your pension centre or Jobcentre Plus office. If you are on universal credit, please call the helpline at 0800 328 9344.

You should receive payment within 14 working days after each period of extremely cold weather in your area.

This will be deposited into the same bank or building society account where your benefit payments are deposited.
