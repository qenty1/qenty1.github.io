      Metro center shooting

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

A person was shot and killed by an off-duty FBI Special agent after an alleged altercation on a Metro platform, according to multiple agencies.

According to a spokesperson for the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority (WMATA), one person was killed in the Metro Center shooting. The identity of that person has not yet been revealed.

Investigators claimed in a press conference Wednesday night that the incident began with an altercation between the person shot and the off-duty FBI special agent. During the fight, the pair fell eight feet over a nearby wall.

After the two fell, the struggle continued, and the agent shot the person shortly after, according to officials.

Following the shooting, the FBI issued a statement, which included the following:

"Any shooting involving our agents or task force members is taken seriously by the FBI. The shooting incident is being investigated by the FBI's Inspection Division, in accordance with FBI policy."

According to WMATA, the officer was taken to a nearby hospital with minor injuries.




Due to police activity, Red Line service was delayed through all downtown stations, and trains were single-tracking. By Thursday morning, all trains were running normally.

No information about the events leading up to the altercation has been released by police.

The shooting occurred on the same night that President Biden spoke at St. Marks Episcopal Church on Capitol Hill at the National Vigil for All Victims of Gun Violence.

This is an ongoing story. More information will be added as it becomes available.
