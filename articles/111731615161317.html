The agent and the other person got into a fight before 6:30 p.m. Wednesday, and the two of them fell about an 8-foot drop over a wall away from the tracks at the end of the platform, according to police. An initial investigation discovered that the struggle continued and that shots were fired.

"Shots were fired by an off-duty, veteran special agent assigned to FBI headquarters," said Ashan Benedict, Executive Assistant Chief of Police.

According to police, the person who was shot has died.

The FBI agent was taken to a hospital for minor injuries, according to police. There is no danger to the Metro system.

Eyewitness Lisa Crawford, like many other passengers, was on her phone when her train arrived at Metro Center and she looked up.

"It felt like a zombie movie," she remarked. "Everyone was fleeing for their lives." Crawford claimed she yelled, "Everyone, get down! Shooting enthusiast!" "I was yelling at the top of my lungs," she admitted.

Crawford, who works as a math teacher at a school near the White House, said an active shooter training video shown at work about a week ago gave her the courage to act. "And then the train took off," she explained.




When the train arrived at Gallery Place, people began assisting others in getting up, and the train operator instructed everyone to disembark, according to Crawford. "People just ran out of the train," she explained.

She and the others eventually got back on the train and resumed their journeys. "It was very frightening; I had never seen anything like it," Crawford said.

According to Benedict, the Metropolitan Police Department is leading the investigation with the assistance of the FBI.

"The FBI takes any shooting incident involving our agents or task force members seriously," according to a statement from the FBI's Washington Field Office.

Detectives are investigating the scene and interviewing witnesses.
