      Washington dc police involved shooting

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

According to police, an off-duty FBI special agent opened fire inside the Metro Center station Wednesday night, killing one person.

Initially, D.C. police stated that they were called to assist another agency with an officer-involved shooting at the station.

Executive Assistant Police Chief Ashan Benedict said the department received a call about a shooting on the Red Line platform at Metro Center around 6:21 p.m.

A preliminary investigation revealed that the special agent and another person got into a fight. The agent and the other person fell over an 8-foot drop behind the platform, away from the tracks, during the scuffle.

The federal agent then opened fire on the other person.

According to Benedict, the person who was shot died at the scene. After suffering minor injuries during the altercation, the FBI agent was taken to the hospital.




Metro General Manager Randy Clarke expressed appreciation for the rail operator's response to the situation in a tweet.

"Really grateful for the rail operator's quick reaction thoughtful attention tonight, whose response during tonight's police-involved shooting at Metro Center kept customers safe," he tweeted. "Metro will assist D.C. police, who are the lead investigator."

Metro has announced that there will be delays at the station as a result of the police investigation. Between Judiciary Square and Farragut North, there will be a single track. Train service between Farragut North and Gallery Place was also halted due to police activity. Shuttle buses have been requested.

According to a statement issued by the FBI Washington Field Office, the agency is "cooperating with MPD to investigate an agent-involved shooting that occurred at Metro Center."

"Any shooting involving our agents or task force members is taken seriously by the FBI. According to FBI policy, the shooting incident is being investigated by the FBI's Inspection Division "According to the statement.
