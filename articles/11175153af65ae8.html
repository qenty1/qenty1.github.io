Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

According to police, an off-duty FBI agent appeared to fatally shoot a person at a metro station in Washington, D.C., on Wednesday night.

D.C. Metro Transit Police responded to reports of multiple shots fired on the Red Line platform at the Metro Center station shortly before 6:30 p.m. Wednesday. According to preliminary reports, the federal officer opened fire, fatally striking one person. The officer was also taken to a local hospital with "unknown injuries," according to police.

Ashan Benedict, executive assistant chief of police for the Metropolitan Police Department, later stated that it appeared two individuals, including the agent, were involved in an altercation in which one apparently grabbed the other and both went over an 8-foot drop on a side wall away from the tracks.

The struggle continued, and shots were fired, according to Benedict.




He added that the FBI agent was taken to a nearby hospital with minor injuries.

Multiple sources told ABC News there is no ongoing threat to the public.

Red Line service has been suspended between Farragut North and Gallery Place amid the investigation and delays are expected in both directions.
