      Off duty fbi agent

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

An off-duty FBI agent fatally shot a man on a crowded Washington D.C. train platform Wednesday evening, according to police.

The special agent and the man fought, falling eight feet off the Metro Center Station platform around 6:20 p.m., before the agent fired several times at the man, killing him, DC police said during a press conference.

According to Ashan Benedict, DC Executive Assistant Chief of Police, the man who was killed appeared to have started the altercation, and that agent, whom he described as a "veteran," is believed to be the victim.

The man "assaulted" the agent, and the two struggled. According to Benedict, one grabbed the other and both tumbled over a wall to a lower walkway away from the tracks.

According to him, the brawl continued on the lower level until the off-duty agent pulled out his gun and shot the other man.

It is unknown whether the man who was killed had a firearm. By the time the press conference was held about two hours later, no gun had been recovered.

The agent was taken to a nearby hospital for minor injuries.




The sound of gunfire echoed through the busy station, causing commuters to panic and suspending service along the Red Line.

According to social media reports, witnesses saw dozens of people sprint out of the station and straphangers drop to the ground after hearing shots.

After the conductor heard the shots, at least one train continued straight through the station without stopping.

"Really grateful for our rail operator's quick response and thoughtful attention tonight, whose response during tonight's police-involved shooting at @wmata Metro Center kept customers safe," Randy Clarke, Metro's General Manager and CEO, tweeted.

DC police are leading the investigation into the shooting with the assistance of the FBI.
