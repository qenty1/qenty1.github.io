      Major incident london

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

Passengers traveling to Manchester from London Euston this evening faced major delays or cancellations due to several trains being delayed or cancelled.

Seven platforms at Euston were closed today, resulting in massive queues. Passengers were evacuated from a train this morning after a 25,000-volt overhead power cable snapped near the station (Wednesday, December 7).

According to My London, the incident has caused chaos throughout the day, with some trains being delayed by two hours. Passengers arrive at Euston station to find their trains cancelled or delayed.

Due to the incident, there are significant delays on Avanti West Coast, London Northwestern, and London Overground services. Among those affected are Avanti West Coast services from Euston to Manchester Piccadilly and Liverpool Lime Street.

Several trains from the capital to Manchester have been canceled, according to the rail operator's website.

One passenger attempting to travel to Manchester captured images of crowds gathered at the station as people waited to board their trains at platform barriers.




According to reports, some people are being denied access to platforms.

Earlier, a Network Rail spokesperson stated: "West Coast main line passengers have been warned of significant delays following the collapse of 25,000-volt overhead cables that power trains outside London's Euston station.

"Engineers from Network Rail are racing to repair the damage to the vital web of wires that was caught on a passing train at 8.50 a.m. this morning.

"Teams have also been working to assist passengers in safely exiting trains that had to be brought to a halt after the electric lines were forced to shut down.

"Because of the damage, no trains are currently serving platforms 1-7 at the major London terminus. Passengers are being advised to check www.nationalrail.co.uk with disruption expected for the whole of today.
