Following a police appeal, a missing Reading man was found. The 82-year-old man, only known as Utter, was last seen in the town's Cemetery Junction area before going missing.

Officers put out an appeal to the public with information about areas that Utter is known to frequent, including the centre of Reading as well as Whitley, Woodley, and Caversham. The 82-year-old was discovered hours after the appeal was published as a direct result of public information.

According to a Thames Valley Police spokesman: "We'd like to thank everyone who shared and responded to our appeal last night to find Utter, who has gone missing. He was discovered safe and well a few hours later, thanks to information we received from the public following the appeal."




According to the charity Missing People, approximately 170,000 people are reported missing to the police in the United Kingdom each year. Nearly 98,000 of the 170,000 people reported missing are adults, with over 70,000 being children.

The vast majority of missing people (80% of missing children and 75% of missing adults) are found or returned within 24 hours. Only a small number of people will be gone for more than a week.
