Ramesh "Sunny" Balwani, the former president of the failed blood-testing company Theranos, was sentenced to 12 years and 11 months in prison on charges of defrauding investors and patients of the Elizabeth Holmes-led blood-testing startup.

Prosecutors claimed that the 57-year-old conspired with Holmes, with whom he had a romantic relationship, to defraud Silicon Valley investors. According to NBC News, US District Court Judge Edward Davila said during Balwani's sentencing hearing in San Jose, California, "They misled investors, they misled patients."

His sentencing comes less than three weeks after Holmes was sentenced to 11 years and three months in prison, to begin in April. In 2018, Holmes was charged alongside Balwani.

Ramesh 'Sunny' Balwani, the company's former chief operating officer, is said to have an estimated net worth of $85 million as of 2022, according to multiple net worth reports. The net worth figure, however, cannot be independently verified.

Balwani, who was born in Sindh, Pakistan, began working for Theranos in 2009. He was married to Keiko Fujimoto, a Japanese artist. Before their divorce in December 2002, the couple lived in San Francisco.




During his time at Theranos, Balwani was in a romantic relationship with Holmes. Balwani, then 37, met Holmes, then 18 and in her senior year of high school. Their relationship was kept from their Theranos investors.

They met for the first time on a language exchange program in Beijing, China. The relationship between Balwani and Holmes was documented in the 2022 miniseries The Dropout. Naveen Andrews portrayed Balwani.

During her trial, Holmes claimed that she had a decade-long relationship with Balwani. He was said to be very controlling and to have sexually abused her.

In her court testimony, Holmes stated that Balwani wanted to "kill the person" she was and make her into a "new Elizabeth."

Balwani and his ex-wife Fujimoto denied the allegations of abuse in court, calling them "false and inflammatory."
