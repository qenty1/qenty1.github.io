      Tamaqua fire today

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

Pennsylvania State Police confirmed memorial plans for the two fallen firefighters, Community Fire Company of New Tripoli Assistant Fire Chief Zachary Paris, 36, and Marvin Gruber, 59.

The body discovered at the scene is still being investigated. The house had two occupants.

Two people were rescued and are being assisted by the Red Cross.

The firefighters who died are Community Fire Company New Tripoli Assistant Fire Chief Zachary Paris, 36, and Marvin Gruber, 59.

There is no new information on the body discovered on the property, which is still being investigated. This is an active crime scene, according to the West Penn Township police chief.

Pennsylvania State Police are assisting on the scene.

A press conference is set to take place soon, and Newswatch 16 will broadcast it live on WNEP.com and WNEP+ on Roku and FireTV.

Newswatch 16 has learned that the two firefighters who died in the hospital as a result of injuries sustained while battling the fire are not from Lehigh County.




The body was discovered about 100 yards behind the house, which sits on a 20-acre plot of land.

Police are not saying whether that body was directly involved in tonight's fire.

Several fire departments from various counties are on the scene. Tankers have been bringing in water as the fire has rekindled in various parts of the house.

After being transported from the fire scene, two firefighters died at the hospital.

Another body was discovered on the property.

The identities of the firefighters and the third person discovered dead are unknown.

No official statement has been issued by the police.
