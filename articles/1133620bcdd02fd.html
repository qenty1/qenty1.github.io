      Penn township fire

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

According to police and the Frederick County Firefighters Union, a Maryland firefighter was among two first responders killed Wednesday while battling a house fire in Pennsylvania where a body was discovered.

According to Pennsylvania State Police Trooper David Beohm, the slain firefighters were members of the New Tripoli Fire Company, Assistant Fire Chief Zachary Paris, 36, and Marvin Gruber, 59. According to authorities, they became trapped inside the house. Firefighters rescued them, but both died later.

Another body was discovered outside the house on the property, which is located in West Penn Township near Tamaqua in Schuylkill County.

According to Pennsylvania State Police, two people who lived in the house escaped safely.

"We have a body in the back, and that's all part of this investigation," Boem explained.




According to West Penn Township Police Chief James Bonner, two people who lived in the three-story single-family home, "an uncle and nephew," were able to escape the fire. Two other firefighters, he said, were treated for injuries.

They reported that more than 100 firefighters and officers arrived shortly before 4 p.m. Bonner described the scene as a "active crime scene," with Pennsylvania State Police and the federal Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, and Firearms assisting with the investigation.

In February, Zachary Paris joined the Frederick County Fire and Rescue Service. In addition, he was a volunteer firefighter in Pennsylvania. According to the Frederick County Firefighters Union, he leaves behind a wife and two daughters.
