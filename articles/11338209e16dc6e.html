      Pennsylvania firefighter death

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

Officials say three people have died as a result of a Schuylkill County fire, including two firefighters.

Several fire departments were dispatched to the 1100 block of Clamtown Road in West Penn Township around 4:00 p.m. Wednesday afternoon.

When first responders arrived at the scene, they discovered one person dead behind the house. The West Penn Township Police Department is investigating their death.

The Schuylkill County Communications Center tells Eyewitness News the fire was upgraded to a third-alarm fire with multiple special units called to the scene.




First responders say two New Tripoli firefighters were trapped inside while attempting to extinguish the flames, and a mayday call was activated.

According to the Schuylkill County Coroner's Office, both firefighters were rescued from the fire but died as a result of their injuries. They have been identified as Assistant Fire Chief Zachary Paris, 36, and Marvin Gruber, 59. The cause of the fire is being investigated by West Penn Township Police and a State Police Fire Marshal.
