A massive house fire in Schuylkill County killed two firefighters who were trapped inside, and officials now say a third body has been discovered.

SkookNews reports that around 3:45 p.m. Wednesday, fire crews were called to the 1100 block of Clamtown Road, Route 443, in West Penn Township, for a single-home fire with possible entrapment.

An hour into the fire, two mayday calls were made by firefighters trapped on the second floor, according to the outlet.

Despite the fact that the firefighters were rescued after 30 minutes, the Schuylkill County Coroner's Office confirmed to SkookNews that both died at a hospital.




According to WFMZ, both firefighters were from Lehigh County. Citizen's Fire Company expressed sympathy to New Tripoli Fire Company for the loss of their members. According to WBRE, the coroner's office was called to the home, and SkookNews reported that it was because a third body was discovered nearby.

According to The Morning Call, the person was a resident who died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound.

According to WBRE, the fire, which was upgraded to a third alarm during the firefight, produced smoke that could be seen for miles.
