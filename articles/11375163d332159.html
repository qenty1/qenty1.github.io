      Walmart stores closing

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

This week, Walmart CEO Doug McMillon warned that rising thefts at the nation's largest retailer could force higher prices and even store closures.

"Theft is a problem. "It's higher than it's ever been," McMillon said during an appearance on CNBC's "Squawk Box.""

When asked Tuesday about how some jurisdictions have moved toward prosecuting theft only above a certain threshold, McMillon warned that approach could have long-term consequences for consumers.

"If that is not corrected over time, prices will rise and/or stores will close," McMillon predicted.

"It's a city by city, location by location thing. Store managers collaborate with local law enforcement, and we have good relationships there for the most part, so that's how we approach it "He continued.

Walmart isn't the only retailer dealing with an increase in shoplifting and large-scale, organized thefts.

Walmart competitor Target said last month that theft was a growing problem in its stores as well, with the number of thefts increasing by 50% this year.

According to Business Insider, while discussing third-quarter earnings results, executives for the Minneapolis-based retailer said organized retail crime has been a major driving force behind hundreds of millions of dollars in profit losses due to inventory shrink in 2022.

According to a 2022 National Retail Federation survey, retailers reported an average 26.5% increase in organized retail crime. WASHINGTON
