      Elon musk ceo tesla

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

Elon Musk, the CEO of Twitter and Tesla (TSLA.O), briefly lost his title as the world's richest person on Wednesday, according to Forbes, due to a sharp drop in the value of his stake in the electric-car maker and a $44 billion bet on the social media firm.

According to Forbes, Bernard Arnault, the CEO of Louis Vuitton's parent company LVMH (LVMH.PA), and his family briefly held the title of world's richest, but fell back to second place with a personal wealth of $185.3 billion.

Musk has a net worth of $185.7 billion and has topped the Forbes list since September 2021. Musk succeeded Jeff Bezos, the founder of Amazon.com (AMZN.O).

Tesla shares were down 2.7% after losing more than 47% of their value since Musk made his offer to buy Twitter earlier this year.




Musk's net worth fell below $200 billion earlier on Nov. 8 as investors dumped Tesla shares amid concerns that the world's most valuable electric-vehicle maker's top executive and largest shareholder is more preoccupied with Twitter.

Tesla's market value has dropped by nearly half, and Musk's net worth has dropped by roughly $70 billion since his April bid for Twitter. Musk completed the Twitter transaction in October, with a $13 billion loan and a $33.5 billion equity commitment.

Musk, in addition to Tesla, is the CEO of SpaceX and Neuralink, a startup that is developing ultra-high bandwidth brain-machine interfaces to connect the human brain to computers.
