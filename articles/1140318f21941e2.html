      Himachal pradesh election 2017

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

Himachal Pradesh Chief Minister Jai Ram Thakur conceded defeat in the hill state on Thursday, as the BJP won 25 seats and was dethroned. According to the final results released by the Election Commission of India, the Congress, which had a 68-seat majority, won 40 of them (ECI).

The winning party with a clear majority in the elections is the party that wins 35 seats. The BJP won 44 seats in the 2017 assembly elections, while the Congress won 21, with one seat going to the CPI(M) and two going to independent candidates. According to the final results updated by the Election Commission of India, the Congress won the Himachal Pradesh assembly election with 40 seats (ECI). The BJP received 25 seats, while the AAP received only five.

In terms of vote share in Himachal, the Congress is only marginally ahead of the BJP, with 43.90% of the vote to the BJP's 42.99%. Others have received 10.4 percent of the vote in the hill state.

The BJP attempted to nip anti-incumbency in the bud in Himachal Pradesh by removing 11 of its 44 MLAs. However, this backfired and resulted in a full-fledged rebellion. Many of the rebels could not be appeased, and it is believed that this cost the party tens of seats, a crucial difference that could have cost it power.

There's also a family feud: Dhani Ram Shandil of the Congress defeats his son-in-law to retain Solan seat

Veteran Congress leader Dhani Ram Shandil of the Congress defeated his nearest BJP rival and son-in-law Rajesh Kashyap to retain the Solan seat in Himachal Pradesh. Dhani Ram Shandil, a retired army colonel and the oldest candidate in the race at 82, led Rajesh Kashyap from the start, and the final margin of victory was 3,858 votes.




Not afraid of poaching': Pratibha Singh takes the lead for the Congress in Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh Congress chief Pratibha Virbhadra Singh said the party is not concerned about poaching, describing the party's victory in the state as a "emotional moment."

"It is a very emotional moment for me because I can see the same kind of support from people as it was for the late Virbhadra Singh," the Congress leader said. We are not concerned about poaching because Chandigarh is an easily accessible meeting place for MLAs."

'Rahul Gandhi's Bharat Jodo Yatra helped...': Mallikarjun Kharge on Himachal results "We have won the elections in Himachal." I'd like to thank the people, our workers, and our leaders for their efforts in achieving this result. I'd like to express my gratitude to Priyanka Gandhi. Rahul Gandhi's Bharat Jodo Yatra also aided us in this regard. "We have Sonia Gandhi's blessings as well," Mallikarjun Kharge said.

Jai Ram Thakur resigns, congratulates the Congress on its victory

Jai Ram Thakur, the outgoing chief minister of Himachal Pradesh, tendered his resignation and congratulated the Congress party on its victory. "I am grateful to the people of Himachal Pradesh for their affection and support for the BJP."
