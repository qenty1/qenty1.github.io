      Election results live 2022

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

Gujarat Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel will be sworn in for a second term on December 12, the party announced Thursday. The inauguration will take place at 2 p.m. on Monday, in the presence of Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Union Home Minister Amit Shah, according to state BJP president C R Paatil. He was speaking at a press conference at the BJP office in Gandhinagar, where party supporters were seen celebrating the party's victory.

Surat East and Limbayat Assembly seats, which fall under the Lok Sabha constituency of state BJP president C R Paatil, saw a tough race. Arvind Rana of the BJP and Aslam Cyclewala of the Congress traded places in Surat East until the 18th round, when Rana won by over 14,000 votes. The Aam Aadmi Party had withdrawn from this seat, which has over 92,000 Muslim voters, leaving the BJP, Congress, and AIMIM to compete. In Surat, Asaduddin Owaisi held three rallies in support of the party's candidate.

After a resurgence in the Saurashtra-Kutch region that gave the ruling BJP a scare five years ago, the Congress bit the dust, winning only three out of 54 seats in the 2022 Assembly elections, the results of which were announced on Thursday. The ruling party made a near-complete sweep of the agrarian region.

The BJP's historic victory in Gujarat is being viewed as a comprehensive victory of the party's macro and micro strategies, with Prime Minister Narendra Modi at the helm.




If Gujarat exemplifies the BJP's current invincibility, with nearly double the vote share of its nearest rival in the state, Himachal Pradesh highlights the difficulties in displacing the BJP. In terms of vote share, the Congress and BJP are virtually tied. Both assembly outcomes indicate that the second dominant party system that emerged in 2014 is not on the decline. In a system dominated by one party, the Opposition's expectations frequently hinge on the ultimate possibility of voter fatigue, rather than voter response to genuine economic woes.
