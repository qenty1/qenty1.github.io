      Harry and meghan docuseries

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

The highly anticipated first three episodes of the six-part series, titled "Harry Meghan," debuted on Netflix on Thursday.

In the docuseries, the Duke and Duchess of Sussex discuss everything from their love story to their decision to step down from senior royal roles two years ago.

The Oscar-nominated Liz Garbus-directed docuseries is billed as a "first-hand account of Harry and Meghan's story, told with never-before-seen personal archive." It begins by noting that the docuseries' interviews were completed by August, one month before Harry's grandmother, Queen Elizabeth II, died.

Members of the British royal family "refused to comment on the content of this series," according to the docuseries.

On Thursday, a Kensington Palace spokesman told ABC News that the royals had not been "approached for comment."

"I can confirm as guidance that no MRF [members of the royal family] were approached for comment on the content of the series," the spokesman said. "I will not comment further on this or any other aspect of it."

Beginning with the first episode of the docuseries, Harry and Meghan criticize the media and its coverage of their relationship and the royal family.

Harry describes a childhood spent being pursued by paparazzi, culminating in the death of his mother, Princess Diana, in a car crash in Paris while being pursued by paparazzi.




"I accept that there will be people all over the world who fundamentally disagree with what I've done and how I've done it, but I knew I had to do everything I could to protect my family, especially after what happened to my mom," Harry said, referring to his and Meghan's decision to step down from senior royal positions. "I didn't want to see history repeat itself."

Harry also expressed concern that media pressure would drive Meghan away, as it had done with previous girlfriends, saying, "I was terrified of her being driven away by the media, the same media that had driven so many people away from me."

Meghan described how her life was turned upside down after meeting Harry, as she was pursued by paparazzi in Toronto, where she claimed neighbors were paid to position a camera in her backyard and police struggled to assist.

""If any other woman in Toronto said to you, 'I have six grown men sleeping in their cars around my house and following me everywhere I go, and I feel scared,' wouldn't you say that was stalking?" she said, adding that police agreed with her but said, "There's really nothing we can do because of who you're dating."

Harry, who has previously taken British tabloids to court, also discusses the royal rota system for covering the royal family, in which different press outlets are given access.

"If you're on the royal rota, you have priority over everyone else when it comes to the story. "All royal news goes through the filter of all newspapers on the royal rota, the majority of which, with the exception of the Telegraph, are tabloids," Harry explained. 'This family is ours to exploit,' I say.
