In the case of Mars, it will appear at 17:06 hours and disappear at 6:37 hours, when it will be at 0.549 Astronomical Units, or 82 million 350 thousand kilometers from Earth.

The coordinator of the ITM's Astronomy Group "Hipatia of Alexandria" announced that the red planet will be completely illuminated, giving it a magnitude of -1.9.




Similarly, it will be in opposition, that is, when the Sun appears on the horizon.

According to the Mexican Association of the Tourism Industry (AMIT) Yucatan's "Tourism Jaguar 2020" award, there will be a conjunction between the Moon and Mars, which means they will be extremely close in the celestial vault.

The closest visual approach will be at 21:09 hours, at a distance of 0.3 degrees of arc.
