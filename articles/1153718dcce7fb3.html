      What star is next to the moon tonight

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

The last full moon of the year will appear on December 7, 2022, making it a special night for astronomy enthusiasts.

Not only will the "Cold Moon" reach maximum illumination on Wednesday at 10:09 p.m. CST, but it will also completely eclipse the planet Mars!

According to the Farmer's Almanac, the full moon in December has a fairly high orbit in the sky, which means it will stay above the horizon for a longer period of time than most full moons.

Experts say the "lunar occultation" can begin just a few hours after sunset, so anyone interested in seeing it should look for Mars earlier in the night and keep a close eye on things.




According to the almanac, Mars will appear as a bright "star" with a reddish glow around it and will be located to the left or lower left of the moon.
