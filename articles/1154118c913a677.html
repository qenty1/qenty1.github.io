      Mars under the moon

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

A rare event in the sky could be seen Wednesday night as the full moon blocks Mars.

The full moon on Wednesday will be the last of 2022, reaching its peak at 11:08 p.m.

A lunar occultation of Mars will occur around 11 p.m., when Mars will disappear behind the full moon. A faint red glow near the edge of the moon may still be visible.




The big question for New Hampshire observers is whether the weather will cooperate. Clouds and showers are expected to clear overnight, but the Lakes Region and points south and east will have the best chance. The prospects for towns in the Upper Valley, White Mountains, and Great North Woods are not promising.

Observers will also be able to see Jupiter and Saturn if the skies are clear.
