      Tik tok banned

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

TikTok use on government devices is being prohibited in a growing number of states due to potential national security threats posed by the Chinese-owned social media platform.

Texas became the latest state to outlaw the popular app on Wednesday, joining Maryland, South Dakota, South Carolina, and Nebraska.

Officials in the United States are concerned that the Chinese government will force TikTok, which is owned by the Chinese company ByteDance, to share data on its millions of users.

"TikTok harvests vast amounts of data from its users' devices and offers this trove of potentially sensitive information to the Chinese government," Texas Gov. Greg Abbott said in a letter announcing the ban.

For years, the US intelligence community has been concerned about how the company's data is being used. Earlier this month, FBI Director Christopher Wray stated that the company's data collection "can be used for traditional espionage operations."

The Trump administration threatened to ban the app unless it was purchased by a US company, citing potential security and privacy risks. President Biden rescinded Trump's ban, but ordered a government review of foreign-owned apps to determine whether they pose any security risks.




The United States military previously prohibited its members from using TikTok on government-issued devices.

TikTok denies sharing information with the Chinese government.

Michael Beckerman, TikTok's head of public policy for North America, told CBS News on Wednesday the company collects data similar to other apps.

"Perhaps they should think about banning all social media apps from government phones," Beckerman suggested.

TikTok added in a statement, "The concerns driving these bans are largely fueled by misinformation about our company."
