      Tik tok chinese

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

Indiana sued TikTok, a Chinese-owned short-video sharing app, on Wednesday, alleging that it misleads users about China's access to their data and exposes children to mature content.

According to the office of Republican Indiana Attorney General Todd Rokita, the popular app, owned by ByteDance, violates the state's consumer protection laws by failing to disclose the Chinese government's potential access to sensitive consumer information.

TikTok also misled young users and their parents with its age rating of 12-plus in Apple's (AAPL.O) and Google's (GOOGL.O) app stores, according to a complaint filed by Rokita's office on Wednesday. According to the complaint, inappropriate sexual and substance-related content is easily accessible and is pushed to children via TikTok by the company.

Indiana claimed its actions were the first of their kind by a state in the United States. Rokita is suing the company for emergency injunctions and civil penalties.

A spokesperson for the video-sharing app said the company had no comment on the ongoing legal action.

Also on Wednesday, Texas Governor Greg Abbott announced that he has directed all Texas state agencies to prohibit the use of TikTok on government-issued devices. Abbott stated on Twitter that the Chinese Communist Party is a growing threat to American cybersecurity.




Indiana and Texas' actions followed a day earlier emergency directive issued by Maryland Governor Larry Hogan prohibiting the use of TikTok on state government devices and networks.

South Dakota Governor Kristi Noem signed an executive order last week prohibiting state employees and contractors from installing or using TikTok on state-owned devices, and South Carolina Governor Henry McMaster requested on Monday that TikTok be removed from state government phones and computers.

Last month, FBI Director Chris Wray expressed concern about TikTok's operations in the United States, citing the possibility that the Chinese government could use the video-sharing app to influence users or control their devices.

Former President Donald Trump attempted to prevent new US users from downloading WeChat and TikTok in 2020, effectively blocking the apps' use in the US, but lost a series of court battles.

In June 2021, President Joe Biden rescinded Trump's executive orders attempting to ban the downloads and directed the Commerce Department to conduct an investigation into the security concerns raised by the apps.
