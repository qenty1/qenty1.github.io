Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

I had assumed that Ken Block's Gymkhana series had run its course. It was epic when it first came out, with the videos looking like gameplay scenes from the Dirt video game series. The things Block was able to make his truly antisocial Ford Focuses do were fascinating. Then he did something strange with a first-generation Ford Mustang, and things got really interesting. Then he drove it in a 1970s Ford F-100 pickup truck in tribute to Climb Dance.

However, after raising the bar so high, the bloom has fallen off the rose in some of the more recent videos, culminating in this fall's Elektrikhana. The multimillion-dollar, one-of-a-kind electric Audi S1 Hoonitron is undeniably cool, but the nearly silent tire shredding in Las Vegas wasn't as exciting as we had hoped.

But then came Gymkhana 2022, starring Travis Pastrana in a wild, 865 hp Family Huckster modified by Vermont SportsCar. Hoonigan, Block, and the Gymkhana series have all returned to form with this release.




There are so many moments in the video that will leave you stammering that trying to pick a standout is pointless. To put it another way: The most impressive part of the video is Pastrana jumping over a helicopter hovering between the spans of an open drawbridge. Seriously. There's so much going on here that a straight-up, 150-mph bridge jump isn't even the most impressive stunt. Go watch the video to see what we mean.

And if none of that is enough to entice you, there are cameos by the likes of YouTube superstar Cleetus McFarland, and Monster Jam star Ryan Anderson in his truck Son-uva Digger. That's all from us. Go watch the video. At the moment. Seriously.
