      Lasik eye surgery news

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

I wear glasses and find the idea of lasers being shot into my eyes unsettling, so I've never had Lasik surgery to improve my vision.

Perhaps it's for the best.

The FDA has issued draft guidance warning that patients undergoing Lasik surgery may experience double vision, dry eyes, difficulty driving at night, and, in some cases, chronic eye pain.

Oh, and the agency says you might still need glasses after the surgery.

The warning may come as a surprise to the roughly 500,000 adults who have Lasik surgery each year, believing there are no risks.

By cutting a thin flap and folding it back, Lasik surgery reshapes the cornea, refocusing how light reaches the retina. The procedure, which costs thousands of dollars, usually takes under 15 minutes per eye.

Lasik surgeons cite surveys indicating that up to 95% of people are satisfied with the results.




Indeed, medical organizations representing surgeons and device manufacturers claim that the FDA is misleading patients by providing only one side of the story.

According to the agency's draft guidance, some Lasik patients have experienced severe depression as a result of complications. According to the FDA, some have even considered suicide.

It's still early. As they prepare a final report, federal officials will consider hundreds of responses.

Me, I'll probably stay away.

My brother, who wears glasses, had Lasik surgery years ago. He expressed his delight with the outcome at the time.

He now wears glasses again.
