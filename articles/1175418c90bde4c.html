      Keystone oil pipeline

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

On Thursday, Canada's TC Energy (TRP.TO) said it had shut down the 622,000-barrel-per-day Keystone pipeline in response to an oil spill in a creek about 20 miles south of Steele City, Nebraska.

The company said in a release that an emergency shutdown and response were initiated around 8 p.m. CT on Dec. 7 after alarms and a pressure drop in the system, and that booms were deployed to control downstream migration of the release.

"The system remains shut down as our crews respond actively and work to contain and recover the oil," according to the release.

The company did not immediately respond to a request for comment on the volume of the release.




Keystone transports Canadian crude from Alberta to the Midwest and then to the Gulf Coast, and is an important component of Canada's oil export network.

On November 15, the company announced that it would reduce pipeline volumes due to some severe weather-related incidents, without specifying the size or duration of the curbs.
