      Keystone pipeline shut down

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

On Thursday, TC Energy Corp. announced the closure of the 622,000-barrel-per-day Keystone XL pipeline due to an oil spill into a Kansas creek.

The magnitude of the leak, which occurred about 20 miles south of a major intersection in Steele City, Nebraska, is currently unknown. Keystone is the primary pipeline that transports Canadian crude from Alberta to refineries in the Midwest and Gulf Coast of the United States, as well as barrels to the U.S. hub in Cushing, Oklahoma.

Keystone shut down the line around 8 p.m. CT on Wednesday (2 a.m. GMT Thursday) after alarms went off and system pressure dropped, the company said in a release, adding that booms were used to contain the creek.

"The system remains shut down as our crews respond actively and work to contain and recover the oil," according to the release.




Two Keystone shippers said TC had not yet informed them of how long the pipeline would be closed. TC declined to comment further, instead directing reporters to its press release.

Personnel from the United States Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration (PHMSA) have been dispatched to the site to investigate the leak near Washington, Kan., a town of about 1,000 people.

On November 15, the company announced that it would reduce pipeline volumes due to severe weather-related incidents, but did not specify the size or duration of the curbs.
