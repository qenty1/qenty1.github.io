      Nebraska oil pipeline

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

TC Energy, the Keystone Pipeline's operator, announced on Thursday that it had shut down the oil pipeline in response to an oil spill in a creek near Steele City, Nebraska.

"In response to a confirmed release of oil into a creek approximately 20 miles (approximately 32 kilometers) south of Steele City, NE," the company said in a statement.

TC Energy was forced to conduct an emergency shutdown, which began late on Wednesday in response to alarms and a detected pressure drop in the system.

"The affected segment has been isolated, and booms have been deployed to control the release's downstream migration," the company said.

"The system remains shut down while our crews respond actively and work to contain and recover the oil," it added.




TC Energy has notified its customers and is actively working with third parties to respond to the incident effectively. The company provided no timeline or date for when oil flows would resume.

The 2,687-mile Keystone Pipeline System connects Alberta's crude oil supplies to US refining markets in Illinois, Oklahoma, and Texas, as well as US crude oil supplies from the Cushing, Oklahoma, hub to refining markets on the US Gulf Coast via the Marketlink Pipeline System.

WTI Crude, the US benchmark oil price, increased by more than 3% after news of the outage of the Keystone Pipeline, which transports approximately 600,000 barrels per day (bpd) of oil from Canada to the US. The EIA reported a 5.2 million barrel drop in US crude inventories for the week ending December 2 on Wednesday. However, there were product builds, with gasoline inventories increasing by 5.3 barrels in the week ending December 2, and middle distillates, which include diesel, increasing by 6.2 million barrels.
