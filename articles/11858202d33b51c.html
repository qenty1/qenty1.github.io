Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

As temperatures drop below zero, thousands of people on low incomes in over 300 postcode districts will receive a 25 Cold Weather Payment.

Overnight temperatures dropped to -9.1C (15F) in the Scottish Highlands and -8.9C in Cumbria.

A cold weather warning for England will be in effect until 09:00 GMT on Monday. The UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) issues it when the weather is cold enough to affect people's health.

The government has made Cold Weather Payments available to eligible people in large parts of England and Wales. It is paid once every seven days, and people can check to see if they will receive it online.

The funds will be distributed in Cumbria, the North East and North West of England, West Yorkshire, Greater Manchester, Birmingham, Coventry, Staffordshire, and Brecon.

In Scotland, those on low incomes and benefits may be eligible for a one-time Winter Heating Payment, which is not conditional on temperature.

Current forecasts call for unseasonably cold temperatures well below average for this time of year in many areas, with the cold weather expected to last into next week.

Heavy snow has caused traffic delays throughout the north east of Scotland, with reports of vehicles becoming stuck on some routes.

The wintry weather also caused flight delays at Aberdeen Airport, as well as disruptions to bus services.

The Met Office has issued several weather warnings, including those for the western and eastern coasts of England and Wales, as well as parts of Northern Ireland and northern Scotland. /

Across much of the UK, overnight temperatures fell to between -6 and -7 degrees Celsius.

According to BBC Weather presenter Chris Fawkes, the cold weather will continue into next week as high pressure over Greenland pushes cold Arctic air across the country.




On Thursday afternoon, "snow showers will continue to affect north and east Scotland," he said.

A few centimetres of snow are expected to fall in the north east of England as well.

Temperatures could drop to -10 degrees Celsius on Thursday night, posing a risk of slippery surfaces due to hard frost.

The Met Office has advised people to exercise caution when walking on ice, with the risk of injuries and slips on icy surfaces increasing on Thursday morning.

Hundreds of homes in Sheffield are still without a gas supply six days after water leaked into the mains.

Josh, 30, who lives in Stannington with his girlfriend and two children, is one of those affected.

Josh wrapped his four-year-old son in five quilts as he tucked him into bed on Tuesday. By midnight, he said the house felt "as cold as Iceland," and he spent an hour trying to warm the boy's room with a tiny electric heater.

"But it wasn't enough," Josh admitted. "He awoke at half past five this morning, crying and cold. What can we do more?"

It is possible that the situation will last until the end of this week, and "possibly longer," before gas is restored to everyone.

The UKHSA has reiterated its long-standing advice for people who are unable to heat every room, advising them to warm the living room during the day and bedrooms just before going to bed, ideally to at least 18 degrees Celsius.
