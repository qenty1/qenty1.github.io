      Washington convention center

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

As African leaders prepare to be hosted by President Joe Biden in the District this December, commuters should prepare as well.

The upcoming U.S.-Africa Leaders Summit will have a flurry of effects for drivers, public transit riders, and pedestrians in the Washington Convention Center area from December 7 to 17.

Street closures in the area will begin on Sunday, December 11 at 4 p.m. and will continue through Monday, December 12 at 6 a.m. The majority of street closures will remain in effect until Saturday, December 17 at 12:01 a.m.

Drivers may also encounter general delays in the area, as well as an increase in pedestrian traffic, and may wish to consider alternative routes.

Metrobus, Metrorail, and MetroAccess services will also be affected in the Washington Convention Center area. Mt. Vernon Square Metro Station will be closed beginning Monday, December 12 at 5 p.m. and will remain closed until Thursday, December 15.

Along with the station closure, DC Police have announced that, due to the security perimeter, shuttle buses to and from Mt Vernon Square will no longer be provided.

"As an alternative, customers are encouraged to use the Gallery Place-Chinatown or Shaw-Howard U stations," they said.

Riders should be aware that Gallery Place is approximately four blocks and a five-to-seven-minute walk from Mt Vernon Square Station.




Customers who use nearby stations or bus routes should allow for extra travel time to account for detours and delays.

Metrobus routes will be detoured beginning Saturday, December 10 at the start of service and will continue until Saturday, December 17 at 12:01 a.m.

Detoured routes include: 70, 79, 74, 63, 64, D4, G8, and S2.

Furthermore, due to the road closures, Metro's real-time service information via BusETA and Metro's Trip Planner may not accurately reflect the detoured service.

Customers using MetroAccess should also be aware that door-to-door service will be unavailable within the security perimeter, and delays may occur due to road closures in the area.

"Efforts will be made to provide drop-off service as close to a location in the restricted area as possible," DC Police said.

Parking restrictions will also go into effect on Wednesday, December 7 and will last until Saturday, December 17. According to DC Police, residents and businesses within the closure area will still be accessible, but cars and pedestrians may be required to go through a security checkpoint before entering the area around the convention center.
