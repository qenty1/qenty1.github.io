      On the beach share price

Published on Dec 11, 2022 by Admin.

Online package holiday seller On the Beach(OTB) expressed concern about the impact of the cost-of-living crisis on travel for Brits in 2023, undermining the company's ongoing Covid recovery.

On the Beach reported that profits and revenues had increased year on year due to a rebound in travel demand, but that they had fallen short of the levels seen in 2019, prior to the pandemic.

Revenues increased from 30.5 million in 2021 to 144.3 million in the fiscal year ending September 30, 2022, with a pre-tax profit of 14.1 million, up from 18.4 million the previous year.

This was less than the 147.5 million revenue and 34.5 million pre-tax profit of 2019.

The key date for investors is now 2023, and there will be nerves as the company effectively admitted that it has no idea how hard holiday demand will be hit as food, fuel, and other necessities skyrocket.

It is unclear how the cost-of-living crisis will affect consumer behavior in 2023,' the company said, adding that "hard decisions made over the last year position the group favorably for another year of growth in 2023."

On the Beach shares fell nearly 13% in early trading to 109p, reducing the company's market capitalization to 160 million.




Interestingly, On the Beach stated that it had begun the fiscal year 2023 with a "healthier overall forward order book" than the equivalent period just before the pandemic in 2020.

On the Beach will also have to deal with the departure of founder and CEO Simon Cooper. He founded On the Beach in 2004 and was instrumental in its growth to become one of the UK's leading online beach holiday retailers. He also owns approximately 5.6% of the company's stock.

Cooper, on the other hand, will not be leaving completely. He intends to step down within the next year, but will remain on the board as a non-executive director and remain actively involved in the business.

Cooper will be replaced by Shaun Morton, chief financial officer since 2020 and instrumental in steering On the Beach through the Covid shutdown.

The transition will take place as soon as an appropriate replacement CFO is found.
